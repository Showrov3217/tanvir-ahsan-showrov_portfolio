Profile

Research-oriented engineering profile

I completed my B.Sc. in Avionics Engineering at Aviation and Aerospace University, Bangladesh (AAUB), where my work gradually moved from avionics coursework into machine learning for biomedical signals and cyber-physical security. My strongest research thread so far has been learning from distributed, noisy, privacy-sensitive data — from EEG recordings to UAV telemetry.

I started with hands-on signal and systems coursework — radar engineering, digital signal processing, communication systems, embedded systems — and carried that into machine learning projects involving EEG classification and wireless-network security. My first major publication benchmarked machine learning models for motor-imagery EEG recognition and was later published in Scientific Reports (Nature Portfolio). I then extended that line into a conference paper on robust epileptic seizure detection using denoising and SE-CNN.

My undergraduate thesis moved toward privacy-preserving AI for autonomous systems: a federated intrusion detection system for UAV networks with quantum-inspired feature extraction, differential privacy, and adversarial robustness evaluation. For PhD work, I want to build on this foundation in EEG / biomedical signal processing, federated learning, trustworthy AI, and security for distributed sensing systems.

Research

Current thesis and research agenda

Undergraduate Thesis

Federated, Privacy-Aware Intrusion Detection for UAV Networks

My thesis studies how a fleet of UAVs can collaboratively learn an intrusion detection model without centralizing raw telemetry. The core problem is that centralized collection creates a privacy and security bottleneck exactly where the system is most vulnerable. I therefore frame intrusion detection as a distributed learning problem under resource constraints, privacy risk, and adversarial attack.

The system combines FedAvg-based federated learning, quantum-inspired kernel feature extraction with Nyström approximation, and differential privacy-aware training, then evaluates robustness under FGSM and PGD attacks. I compare centralized and federated setups using accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, ROC-AUC, communication overhead, convergence behavior, and privacy-security tradeoffs.

Federated Learning

Distributed model updates across UAV / FANET nodes without raw-data sharing.

Privacy Layer

Differential privacy-aware design and secure-aggregation-compatible training logic.

Feature Extraction

Quantum-inspired kernel pipeline with Nyström approximation for non-IID telemetry.

Robustness Testing

FGSM / PGD adversarial stress testing to evaluate resilience under evasion attacks.
